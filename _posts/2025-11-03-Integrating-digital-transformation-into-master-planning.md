The digital transformation of our community has emerged as a critical agenda, significantly impacting various aspects of our daily lives. As technology evolves at an extraordinary rate, it presents both opportunities and challenges that warrant careful consideration. This transition goes beyond merely adopting new technologies; it necessitates a reevaluation of how we engage with the world around us.  

Here are several key points from Michigan State University Extension Citizen Planner, related to considerations for integrating digital transformation into the master planning process:

## The Digital Era

### Conversion to Digital Form

- Processes, roles, business operations, social interactions, behaviors, and business models are being digitized.
### Governance and Planning Challenges

- Rapid advancements in science and technology outpace governance and planning capabilities.


## Community and Infrastructure
### Community Engagement
- Importance of retaining and attracting residents to support digital initiatives.
### Broadband Infrastructure
- Critical for supporting digital transformation.
## Planning Implications
### Housing and Development
- Emergence of digital nomads.
- Changes in transportation and mobility needs.
- Evolution of downtown and office districts.
- Essential broadband infrastructure.
### Redevelopment Needs
- Vacant shopping malls and centers.
- Warehouses and fulfillment centers.
- Retaining and attracting people is crucial.
---
These points highlight the significant impact of digital transformation on community planning, infrastructure, and engagement. Leveraging technology for better community planning can help communities build a vision for more resilient and adaptable to future changes through digital transformation.
*Michigan State University Extension. (2025). Citizen Planner: Session 5 - Using Innovative Planning and Zoning. Michigan State University.*
