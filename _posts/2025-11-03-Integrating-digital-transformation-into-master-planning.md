The digital transformation of communities is reshaping how we plan, build, and engage. Technology-driven change requires not only new tools, but also updated planning approaches to infrastructure, governance, and community engagement.

Key considerations from Michigan State University Extension Citizen Planner for integrating digital transformation into master planning:

## The digital era

### Conversion to digital form
- Processes, roles, business operations, social interactions, behaviors, and business models are increasingly digitized.
- Planning should account for digital service delivery, data-driven decision making, and digital equity.

### Governance and planning challenges
- Rapid advances in science and technology often outpace governance and planning capabilities.
- Plans must be adaptable and include mechanisms for iterative policy updates.

## Community and infrastructure

### Community engagement
- Digital tools expand engagement opportunities but require strategies to include digitally underserved populations.
- Retention and attraction of residents remains critical to support local initiatives.

### Broadband infrastructure
- Reliable, high-speed broadband is foundational for digital transformation across sectors (education, business, health, municipal services).

## Planning implications

### Housing and development
- Increasing prevalence of digital nomads and remote work affects housing demand and neighborhood design.
- Changes in transportation and mobility patterns should be anticipated.
- Downtowns and office districts may need reimagining for mixed uses and flexible spaces.
- Broadband access is an essential component of new development standards.

### Redevelopment needs
- Vacant shopping centers and underused commercial buildings present opportunities for adaptive reuse (housing, fulfillment, community space).
- Warehouses and fulfillment centers influence land use and logistics planning.
- Strategies to retain and attract people are central to successful redevelopment.

Integrating digital transformation into master planning means building resilience, flexibility, and equity into plans so communities can adapt as technologies and behaviors evolve.

*Source: Michigan State University Extension. (2025). Citizen Planner: Session 5 - Using Innovative Planning and Zoning.*
