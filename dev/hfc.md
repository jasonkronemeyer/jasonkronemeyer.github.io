In the heart of Hartford, a community in southwest Michigan, the journey towards digital transformation and equity has been a story of resilience, collaboration, and continuous growth. This narrative is deeply intertwined with the introduction of the Compass Series by Compass Communities, a framework designed to guide and support communities in overcoming digital capacity barriers.

## The Power of Yet in Community Growth

The concept of the "Power of Yet," introduced by Carol Dweck, has been a cornerstone in Hartford's approach to digital transformation. This philosophy encourages viewing challenges as opportunities for growth and learning. In Hartford, this mindset has been pivotal in addressing significant capacity barriers such as limited digital infrastructure, lack of digital literacy, and insufficient resources. The community embraced the idea that while they might not have the capacity now, they can develop it over time with persistence and collaboration[1](https://jasonkronemeyercom.sharepoint.com/sites/BTAProjectCompass/Shared%20Documents/The%20Power%20of%20Yet%20in%20Community%20Growth%20-%20Navigatiing%20Community%20Capacity%20Barriers%20for%20Digital%20Transformation.pdf?web=1).

The **Digital Opportunities Compass**, a comprehensive framework developed by experts including Colin Rhinesmith, Pierrette Renée Dagg, Johannes M. Bauer, Greta Byrum, and Aaron Schill, provided Hartford with a structured map to navigate their digital equity journey. This framework is organized across six domains: Contexts, Governance, Connectivity, Skills, Applications, and Outcomes. Each domain plays a vital role in ensuring that investments in digital infrastructure and skills development are paired with the human and institutional capacity required for lasting, meaningful results[1](https://jasonkronemeyercom.sharepoint.com/sites/BTAProjectCompass/Shared%20Documents/The%20Power%20of%20Yet%20in%20Community%20Growth%20-%20Navigatiing%20Community%20Capacity%20Barriers%20for%20Digital%20Transformation.pdf?web=1).

## Hartford's Community Engagement and Strategic Planning

Hartford's journey began with a thorough assessment of local aspirations and resources. Community leaders, residents, and organizations were actively involved in this process, ensuring that digital equity strategies were tailored to the community's unique context. This collaborative engagement allowed residents to voice their needs and aspirations, aligning digital initiatives with the specific circumstances and ambitions of the community[1](https://jasonkronemeyercom.sharepoint.com/sites/BTAProjectCompass/Shared%20Documents/The%20Power%20of%20Yet%20in%20Community%20Growth%20-%20Navigatiing%20Community%20Capacity%20Barriers%20for%20Digital%20Transformation.pdf?web=1).

The **Hartford Joint Community Master Plan 2015-2025** highlighted the community's strengths, opportunities, and goals. It emphasized the importance of promoting economic activities, maintaining and expanding public infrastructure, and fostering cooperation between the city and township. One of the key goals was to expand broadband internet access, recognizing its critical role in modern community development[2](https://jasonkronemeyercom.sharepoint.com/sites/BTAProjectCompass/Shared%20Documents/Hartford/hartford_city_township_masterplan_2015_2025.pdf?web=1).

## Overcoming Barriers and Building Capacity

In Hartford, overcoming barriers such as language, limited digital skills, and connectivity issues has been a collective effort. Community members have found innovative solutions and supported each other through various initiatives. For example, establishing public wireless access points in community spaces, libraries, and schools has been a significant step towards improving digital infrastructure. Additionally, simple yet effective digital literacy programs have been initiated to equip individuals with fundamental skills for navigating the digital world[1](https://jasonkronemeyercom.sharepoint.com/sites/BTAProjectCompass/Shared%20Documents/The%20Power%20of%20Yet%20in%20Community%20Growth%20-%20Navigatiing%20Community%20Capacity%20Barriers%20for%20Digital%20Transformation.pdf?web=1).

The **Digital Opportunities Compass** framework has been instrumental in this process, providing a comprehensive set of metrics to monitor, evaluate, and guide digital transformation efforts. By leveraging this framework, Hartford has developed logic models for program evaluation and adopted a holistic approach to achieving digital equity[1](https://jasonkronemeyercom.sharepoint.com/sites/BTAProjectCompass/Shared%20Documents/The%20Power%20of%20Yet%20in%20Community%20Growth%20-%20Navigatiing%20Community%20Capacity%20Barriers%20for%20Digital%20Transformation.pdf?web=1).

## Continuous Learning and Personal Growth

Continuous learning and personal growth have been central to Hartford's digital transformation journey. Workshops and training sessions aimed at enhancing digital literacy and skills have been regularly conducted, supporting community members in their personal development. These learning opportunities have not only improved digital skills but also fostered a sense of empowerment and fulfillment among participants[1](https://jasonkronemeyercom.sharepoint.com/sites/BTAProjectCompass/Shared%20Documents/The%20Power%20of%20Yet%20in%20Community%20Growth%20-%20Navigatiing%20Community%20Capacity%20Barriers%20for%20Digital%20Transformation.pdf?web=1).

The relationship between self-actualization and aspiration has been evident in Hartford's approach. Aspiration served as a motivator, propelling individuals towards self-actualization. As community members worked towards their digital equity goals, they moved closer to realizing their full potential and achieving self-actualization. This continuous process of personal growth and meaningful contributions to digital equity efforts has been a testament to the community's resilience and determination[1](https://jasonkronemeyercom.sharepoint.com/sites/BTAProjectCompass/Shared%20Documents/The%20Power%20of%20Yet%20in%20Community%20Growth%20-%20Navigatiing%20Community%20Capacity%20Barriers%20for%20Digital%20Transformation.pdf?web=1).

## Conclusion

The story of Hartford and the Compass Series is one of hope, collaboration, and continuous improvement. By embracing the "Power of Yet" and leveraging the Digital Opportunities Compass, Hartford has systematically monitored, evaluated, and guided their digital equity efforts. This framework, combined with fostering collaboration through community engagement, investing in digital literacy, creating supportive environments, and setting incremental goals, has enabled Hartford to overcome challenges and work towards achieving their aspirations for digital equity. With dedication and perseverance, Hartford continues to thrive in the digital age, demonstrating that progress is always possible with the right mindset and collective effort.
